# Research update: 2026-06-29

Purpose: source-backed update for the public map. This separates broad, published reporting from user-submitted video signals.

## High-confidence updates

- Nationwide spread: public reporting now supports treating the crisis as a multi-region national shortage, not isolated local queues. RFE/RL reported restrictions or company-level limits in 55 of 83 federal entities, with additional local reports in nearly two dozen more regions.
- Official acknowledgement: Putin publicly acknowledged a fuel deficit/shortage and linked it to Ukrainian strikes on energy infrastructure, while saying the situation was manageable.
- Crimea: reporting supports a critical Crimea status. Fuel sales to private individuals and businesses were halted, with state agencies exempted.
- Irkutsk Oblast: 50 L/day cap at Rosneft stations remains a strong point signal for Siberian rationing.
- Slavyansk-on-Kuban: AP and other public reporting support a refinery-fire/infrastructure-damage signal.
- Moscow supply zone: treat as a serious supply-risk region after refinery hits, but keep station-level queue claims separate until video/location evidence is available.
